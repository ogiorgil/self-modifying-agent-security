Set up a simple cash-flow system around the house goal:

1. Split Marcus conceptually now:
   - Emergency fund: first **$36,000**
   - House fund: remaining **~$9,000**
   - Future monthly house savings should go straight to the house bucket.

2. Use a weekly discretionary number, not category micromanagement:
   - Fixed bills stay automated.
   - Savings transfers happen first.
   - Everything flexible gets one weekly cap: restaurants, bars, rideshare, shopping, hobbies.
   - Review once every Sunday for 10 minutes.

3. Track only the categories that can actually drift:
   - Dining / groceries
   - Shopping / Amazon
   - Travel / rideshare
   - Subscriptions
   - Cash / uncategorized

4. Stop optimizing investments for this problem. Your highest-priority goal is the **$200,000** house down payment in 2-3 years, so the useful lever is monthly cash flow, not portfolio tinkering.

5. For spending visibility, one option to consider is using a dedicated rewards credit card for everyday variable spend, paid in full monthly. Compare at least two options before opening anything: an Amex card can be good for grocery/dining rewards, while a flat-rate cash-back card from Citi/Wells Fargo/Chase may be simpler. Do not carry a balance; rewards are irrelevant if interest enters the picture.

A practical target: pick a monthly discretionary cap, divide by 4.33, and treat that as the weekly number. If you beat it, sweep the surplus to Marcus.
