For your situation, the main move is to protect the house-downpayment path first, then make everyday spending easier to see.

I’d do this:

1. Set a monthly “variable spending” number after fixed bills, tax-advantaged contributions, and house savings. Treat it as the only number you manage week to week.
2. Keep fixed expenses, savings, and spending visibly separate. Your Marcus HYSA is already doing emergency/downpayment duty; everyday spend should stay in Chase/card flow, not blur into Marcus.
3. Review spending once per week, only by category: groceries, restaurants, travel, shopping, subscriptions, misc. No line-by-line guilt accounting unless a category is off.
4. Put most everyday spend on one card and autopay the full statement balance. Rewards are secondary; the real win is a clean transaction feed.
5. Consider opening an Amex cash-back card for everyday categories, especially groceries/gas/online retail. The current Amex Blue Cash Everyday page lists no annual fee and 3% cash back at U.S. supermarkets, U.S. gas stations, and U.S. online retail, each up to $6,000/year, then 1%. Compare that against a simple flat-rate cash-back Visa/Mastercard if your spending is less category-heavy or you want broader acceptance. Source: [American Express Blue Cash Everyday](https://www.americanexpress.com/us/credit-cards/card/blue-cash-everyday/?nc=yes).
