For everyday spending, I’d make this a cash-flow system, not a budgeting personality project.

Start with a 90-day audit:
1. Export Chase + credit card transactions.
2. Split spending into: fixed bills, groceries, restaurants/delivery, transport, travel, shopping, subscriptions, misc.
3. Ignore one-off annual items at first; find the recurring monthly leak.

Then set a simple monthly structure:
- Keep Chase Checking around its current purpose: `~$8,000` operating float.
- Preserve Marcus HYSA for emergency fund + house downpayment. The first `~$36,000` is conceptually emergency fund; don’t let discretionary spending pull from it.
- Use one “flex spend” number per month for restaurants, shopping, entertainment, and small impulse purchases. Track that weekly, not daily.

Practical rule I’d use: because the house downpayment is the highest priority, every recurring spending reduction should be translated into “months pulled forward” on the `$200,000` downpayment target. Example: cutting `$500/month` of restaurants/subscriptions is meaningful because it funds more than 10% of the planned `$4,500/month` Marcus contribution.

I would not start by opening a new card. Rewards optimization comes after you know the categories. If it becomes relevant, compare at least two options by your actual spend categories and fees.
