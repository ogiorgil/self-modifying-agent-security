You should manage everyday spending with a lightweight guardrail system, not a rigid budget.

Given your current setup, I’d do this:

1. Keep fixed goals automatic first: 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth, emergency fund, and house downpayment savings stay protected.
2. Split Marcus mentally or operationally into two buckets: first **$36,000** emergency fund, anything above that house downpayment. Right now Marcus has **~$45,000**, so only **~$9,000** is truly downpayment progress unless you separate the buckets.
3. Create one monthly “flex spend” number for groceries, restaurants, shopping, travel, subscriptions, and random purchases. Don’t category-budget every dollar at first.
4. Review transactions once a week for 10 minutes. Tag only three things: `needs`, `wants`, `regret`. The “regret” category is the useful one.
5. Add a 24-hour rule for nonessential purchases over a threshold, say `$150` or `$250`.
6. Audit subscriptions quarterly. Kill anything you would not re-buy today.
7. Use credit cards only if paid in full monthly. CFPB guidance is clear that paying in full avoids interest when a grace period applies, and tracking spending is useful for understanding affordability. Sources: [CFPB spending assessment](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/owning-a-home/prepare/assess-your-spending/), [CFPB credit card grace periods](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-grace-period-for-a-credit-card-en-47/).

The main target is not “spend less everywhere.” It’s to protect the house timeline. If you want the downpayment plan to stay on track, every recurring lifestyle increase should be tested against the future **$4,500/month** Marcus contribution plan.
