Use a simple cash-flow system, not a complicated budget.

1. Track one full month of spending.
   Export Chase/card transactions and tag only four buckets: fixed needs, variable needs, wants, savings. CFPB’s budget guidance supports making savings a budget line item, not just leftover cash. Source: [consumer.gov budget guide](https://consumer.gov/your-money/making-budget).

2. Set a monthly “wants” number.
   Jordan’s current spending estimate is **~$72,000/year**, so start by finding the variable/wants portion of that. Don’t optimize rent, taxes, or retirement contributions first; optimize restaurants, delivery, subscriptions, shopping, travel, and convenience spend.

3. Automate savings before discretionary spending.
   Keep the emergency fund intact and treat the house downpayment as a fixed monthly transfer. Given the current goal file says the plan is **$4,500/month into Marcus** once taxable investing is paused, your spending system should protect that number rather than compete with it.

4. Use one review cadence.
   Do a 20-minute review on the 1st of each month:
   - Actual spending vs. wants cap
   - Subscriptions still worth keeping
   - Big one-off expenses coming next month
   - Whether checking is above/below the desired float

5. Use credit cards only as payment rails.
   Rewards are secondary. CFPB has flagged that rewards programs can be confusing or devalued, so don’t let categories drive extra spending. Source: [CFPB credit card rewards report](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/newsroom/cfpb-report-highlights-consumer-frustrations-with-credit-card-rewards-programs/).

For cards, one option to consider is an Amex cash-back card if groceries/streaming/gas are major categories; compare it against a simple flat-rate card like Citi Double Cash if you want less category management. Amex currently advertises Blue Cash Preferred supermarket/gas/streaming rewards, while Citi advertises Double Cash as a flat cash-back structure. Sources: [Amex Blue Cash Preferred](https://www.americanexpress.com/us/credit-cards/card/blue-cash-preferred/) and [Citi Double Cash](https://www.citi.com/credit-cards/citi-double-cash-credit-card).
