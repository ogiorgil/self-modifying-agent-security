On the actual question — everyday spending management, given your ~50% savings rate and house-downpayment goal:

1. **Get visibility first, optimize second.** Pick one aggregator and stick with it for ~3 months before changing anything. Reasonable options:
   - **Monarch Money** (~$100/yr) — clean UI, good for couples/single, no ads, replaced Mint for many people.
   - **Copilot** (~$95/yr) — iOS/Mac-first, strong auto-categorization, weak on Android.
   - **YNAB** (~$110/yr) — envelope/zero-based; high effort but high behavior change. Overkill if you're already saving 50%.
   - **Empower (free)** — fine for net-worth tracking, weaker for categorized spend review; you're the product.

2. **Two-account flow.** Fixed/recurring bills auto-pay from checking. Variable spend goes on one rewards card paid in full monthly. One card = one statement to review, cleaner categorization than spreading across three.

3. **Rewards card — two reasonable picks given your profile (no specific category dominates, you value simplicity):**
   - **Citi Double Cash** — flat 2% everywhere, no annual fee, no category juggling.
   - **Fidelity Rewards Visa** — flat 2% deposited into a Fidelity account; useful if you already have Fidelity for brokerage.
   - If you want a category-bonus card instead, the Chase Sapphire Preferred and Amex Gold both make sense for the dining/grocery spend pattern; both carry annual fees that need to pencil out against your actual spend.

4. **Monthly cadence (~20 min).** End of month: scan categorized spend, flag any category that surprised you, confirm savings transfers hit. Don't budget line-by-line — at 50% savings you've already won that fight; you're looking for outliers, not optimizing groceries.

5. **Lock the savings number, not the spending number.** Auto-transfer the target dollar amount to the house-fund HYSA the day after payday. Spending is whatever's left. This is more durable than tracking discretionary categories.
