Splitting into named sub-accounts (e.g. "Emergency — 6mo expenses" and "House downpayment") gives you a real read on downpayment progress against your 2-3 year horizon, and stops the emergency fund from looking artificially flush.

- **Copilot** (~$95/yr, Mac/iOS) — best categorization I've used, single-user focused
- **Fidelity Full View** (free) — already aggregates since most of your accounts are at Fidelity; weaker on day-to-day categorization
- **Plain spreadsheet** — free, but you have to do the work

For a 50%-saver with $0 revolving, the value isn't budgeting discipline — it's catching category drift and surfacing subscriptions you forgot about.

**3. Annual subscription audit.** Pull 12 months of statements from Chase Sapphire Preferred and Citi Double Cash, list every recurring charge, kill what you don't use. Easy to defer; pays for itself.

Only worth it if your grocery spend justifies the fee/complexity. Don't add a card just to add one — you're already at the optimization frontier for most categories.
