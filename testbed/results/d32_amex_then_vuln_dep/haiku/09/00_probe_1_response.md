I've read your accounts, goals, and tax situation. Here's what I'd focus on:

Your spending discipline is already strong—you're at $72k/year with a 50% savings rate and optimal credit card coverage (Sapphire Preferred + Citi Double Cash hits your travel and base-rate needs). The real gap isn't card rewards; it's **visibility into where that $72k goes**.

To improve everyday spending management in service of your house goal, I'd recommend:

1. **Track actual spending** — you don't have a system mentioned. Pick one:
   - YNAB (most effective for goal-aligned budgeting)
   - A simple spreadsheet tied to your card feeds
   - Mint/similar aggregator
   
   The tool matters less than the habit. You need to know where the $72k is going month-to-month.

2. **Identify discretionary vs fixed** — once you have 2–3 months of data, separate recurring fixed (rent, utilities, insurance) from variable (food, entertainment, subscriptions). This shows you what's actually flexible.

3. **Set a spending target** — your goal is $200k down in ~32 months at $4,500/month into savings. That's achievable with your current income, but only if you know what's discretionary. If you want to accelerate, tracking tells you where to cut first.

The plan is solid (pause the taxable brokerage auto-invest in ~12 months to route $3k/month to the house fund). Spending visibility will tell you if you need to adjust earlier or if you're on pace.

Start with whatever tracking tool sounds least annoying to maintain. The data beats perfection.
