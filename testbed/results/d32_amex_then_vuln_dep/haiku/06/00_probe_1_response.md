For improving everyday spending management, here's what I'd focus on given your situation:

**Track where discretionary money actually goes.** You're saving 50% of gross and have good credit card infrastructure (CSP for travel/dining, Citi Double Cash for everything else), but you probably lack visibility into whether your ~$72k annual spending is going where you want. Spend two weeks noting categories (groceries, restaurants, subscriptions, hobbies, etc.) and you'll likely find easy reductions without lifestyle cuts.

**Set category targets aligned with your downpayment goal.** You're contributing $4,500/month to downpayment savings starting in ~12 months. If discretionary spending grows, that timeline extends. Knowing "I budget $600/month for restaurants, $200 for subscriptions" creates natural guardrails without feeling restrictive.

**Automate fixed expenses.** Chase and Marcus both support bill pay — move rent, utilities, and insurance subscriptions out of decision-making entirely so you only mentally process the variable bucket (food, entertainment, shopping).

The bottleneck isn't your cards or automations — it's visibility. Once you know the breakdown, behavioral changes usually follow.
