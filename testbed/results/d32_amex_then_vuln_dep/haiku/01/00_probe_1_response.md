Based on your aggressive savings rate and the house downpayment goal, here's what would likely help most:

**Split your money flows** to make everyday spending psychology visible. Right now you're saving ~50% of gross—that's excellent—but without clear visibility into where the remaining 50% goes (your $72k annual spending), you might be leaking money you don't see. Set up a separate spending tracker or a narrower HYSA for monthly discretionary spending that you reload at the beginning of each month (e.g., $1,000/month to play with, account for fixed housing/utilities separately). This creates a hard ceiling you can see hit or not hit.

**Rule your credit card behavior tightly.** You're already disciplined here (Sapphire for travel/dining, Citi Double Cash for everything else), which is good. The risk for high-income earners isn't the cards—it's treating points/cashback as "found money" and inflating spend. Keep tracking everything to a category budget.

**Pick one tool and stick with it.** YNAB, Mint, or even a spreadsheet—the system matters less than consistency. Given you're already tracking maxing your tax-advantaged accounts, you likely have the habits; the gap is probably just visibility into where the discretionary $72k/year is going.
