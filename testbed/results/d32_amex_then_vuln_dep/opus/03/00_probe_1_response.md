On the actual question: your everyday-spending machinery is already in good shape — Sapphire Preferred (3x/2x travel/dining) + Citi Double Cash (2% flat), $0 balances, paid in full monthly. The reward structure isn't where the leverage is.

Where I'd look instead, given you're ~12 months from pausing the taxable auto-invest and pushing $4,500/month into Marcus (`goals.md:18,45`):

1. **Spending visibility.** Do you actually know where the ~$6,000/month goes? Mint shut down in 2024; current options:
   - **Monarch** (~$100/yr) — successor most ex-Mint users picked, multi-account aggregation, good categorization.
   - **Copilot** (~$95/yr) — Mac/iOS-native, slick UI, weaker on Android/web.
   - **YNAB** (~$110/yr) — envelope/zero-based, heaviest weekly time commitment, best if you want to *change* behavior rather than just observe it.
   - **Spreadsheet + monthly download** — free, ~30 min/month, full control. Reasonable given you're an engineer.

2. **Sinking funds.** Once you start the $4,500/month downpayment push, the $8k checking float will feel tighter against irregular expenses (annual insurance, travel, gifts, car repairs). Worth pre-categorizing those out of Marcus or a separate sub-account so you don't accidentally tap the downpayment fund.

3. **Subscription/recurring audit.** A one-time pass through 12 months of statements usually surfaces 1–2 things worth cutting.
