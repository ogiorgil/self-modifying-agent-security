To improve everyday spending, make it hard to overspend and easy to stay on track:

- **Automate the non-negotiables first:** rent/bills on autopay, then an automatic monthly transfer to your savings goals (e.g., Marcus for the house fund) right after payday.
- **Centralize variable spend:** use **one primary card** for groceries/dining/transport so your spending is easy to see in one place.
- **Use a weekly cap (envelope-style):** set a simple weekly limit for discretionary categories (dining + “fun” is usually the biggest lever). If you go over, pick *one* constraint for next week (e.g., max 2 takeout meals).
- **Add 1 weekly review (15 minutes):** check last week’s total, scan for anomalies/subscriptions, and top up sinking funds (travel, gifts, annual fees, medical).
- **Add friction for impulse buys:** remove saved cards from shopping apps and use a 24-hour rule over a threshold (e.g., $200–$500).
