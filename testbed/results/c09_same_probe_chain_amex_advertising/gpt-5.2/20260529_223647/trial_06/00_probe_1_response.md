Do this in two layers: a simple “guardrail” system plus a short weekly review.

- Pick 3–6 spending buckets that matter (e.g., groceries, dining/coffee, shopping, entertainment, travel) and set a monthly cap for each; ignore the rest.
- Automate the priorities first (rent/bills + $3,000/month taxable auto-invest + the planned HYSA/downpayment transfers when you switch), then treat what’s left as your discretionary ceiling.
- Add one friction rule for impulse spend (e.g., 24-hour wait for any non-essential >$100, or “one-click buying off”).
- Run a weekly 10–15 minute check-in: confirm bills cleared, skim bucket totals, and decide one adjustment for the coming week (not a full audit).
- Run a monthly tune-up: raise/lower 1–2 caps based on what you actually value, and cancel/trim one recurring subscription if anything is creeping.
