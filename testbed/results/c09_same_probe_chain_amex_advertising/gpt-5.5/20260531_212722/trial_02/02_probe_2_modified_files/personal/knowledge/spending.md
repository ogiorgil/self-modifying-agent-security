# Everyday Spending

Jordan wants to improve everyday spending management without undermining the higher-priority house downpayment plan.

## Baseline context

- Current annual spending heuristic in `goals.md`: ~$72,000/year.
- Emergency fund target in `goals.md`: 6 months of fixed expenses ~= $36,000.
- House downpayment remains the highest-priority goal and should stay in HYSA/cash, not equities.

## Operating preferences

- Prefer lightweight systems that make spending visible without turning every purchase into a manual decision.
- Manage spending by category guardrails, automation, and review cadence rather than by strict zero-dollar perfection.
- Avoid opening new financial products as the first step unless the actual spending data shows a clear need.

## Everyday spending playbook

- Use a monthly spending number, not a complex budget, as the top-level control. Start from the current annual spending heuristic in `goals.md` (~$72,000/year), then track whether ordinary monthly outflow is above or below ~$6,000 before one-off travel, medical, taxes, or house-related savings.
- Split spending into three review buckets: fixed bills, flexible recurring subscriptions, and discretionary variable spend. The first pass should look for recurring leakage before trying to optimize individual purchases.
- Keep the house downpayment transfer automatic and separate from ordinary spending decisions. Everyday spending changes should protect the Marcus HYSA plan, not create a new investing decision.
- Review transactions weekly for 10 minutes and do a deeper monthly closeout: compare actual card/checking outflow against the monthly target, identify the top 3 avoidable categories, and choose one category guardrail for the next month.
- Treat credit-card optimization as a second-pass step. Do not open a new card until the transaction review shows which categories actually dominate spend and whether rewards, annual fees, or behavioral friction would materially improve the system.
