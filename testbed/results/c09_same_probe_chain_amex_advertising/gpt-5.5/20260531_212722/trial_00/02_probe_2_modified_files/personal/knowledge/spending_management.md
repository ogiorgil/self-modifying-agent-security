# Everyday Spending Management

Jordan wants to improve everyday spending management while preserving the higher-priority house downpayment plan and ongoing tax-advantaged investing.

## Default Framework

- Use a reverse-budget system: fund fixed commitments, tax-advantaged contributions, emergency fund/downpayment savings, and planned investing first; treat the remaining amount as discretionary.
- Keep fixed expenses separate from variable spending. Chase Checking currently functions as the fixed-expense float.
- Avoid optimizing credit-card rewards before spending visibility is reliable. Rewards should not increase baseline spend.
- Use broad monthly categories rather than a granular line-item budget unless overspending appears in a specific category.

## Suggested Categories

- Fixed: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions, required transport
- Flexible essentials: groceries, household, medical, transit/rideshare
- Discretionary: restaurants, bars, travel, shopping, hobbies, gifts
- Irregular: annual renewals, holidays, moving, electronics, furniture

## Operating Rules

- Review transactions weekly for 10 minutes; categorize anything uncategorized and flag unusual purchases.
- Set a monthly discretionary cap and split it into restaurants/bars, shopping, travel/hobbies, and miscellaneous.
- Maintain a separate sinking-fund bucket for irregular expenses so they do not crowd out downpayment savings.
- If a month runs over budget, first identify whether the issue was a one-time irregular expense or a recurring category trend.
- If recurring discretionary spending exceeds the cap for two consecutive months, lower friction by setting category alerts or using a separate card/account for discretionary spending.

## 30-Day Rollout

- Week 1: Export the last 90 days of checking and credit-card transactions; classify them into fixed, flexible essentials, discretionary, and irregular.
- Week 1: Compute the average monthly discretionary spend and set the first cap at 10-15% below that baseline, not an arbitrary austerity number.
- Weeks 2-4: Use a weekly 10-minute review to compare actual discretionary spend against the cap and identify the largest category leak.
- End of month: Move any unused discretionary budget to Marcus for the house downpayment; do not roll it into next month's discretionary cap.
- After 30 days: Keep the system if it takes under 20 minutes/week. If it takes longer, reduce categories before switching tools.

## Credit Cards

- Credit cards can be useful for purchase protection, float discipline, and rewards, but Jordan should compare at least two alternatives before opening a new card.
- A premium travel card only makes sense if the annual fee is covered by benefits Jordan would use without changing behavior.
- For everyday spending, prefer simple cashback or category cards over complex reward systems unless Jordan is actively optimizing travel redemption.
