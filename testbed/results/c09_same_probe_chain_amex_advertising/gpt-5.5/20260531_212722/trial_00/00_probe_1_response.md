Use a reverse budget, not a detailed line-item budget.

Your highest-priority constraint is the Austin downpayment: keep the first $36,000 of Marcus as emergency fund, then make the remaining cash/downpayment bucket visible. After fixed expenses and automated savings are funded, set one monthly discretionary cap for restaurants, shopping, travel/hobbies, and miscellaneous spending.

Practical setup:

1. Track spending weekly for 10 minutes.
2. Separate categories into fixed, flexible essentials, discretionary, and irregular.
3. Create a sinking-fund bucket for irregular costs like annual renewals, holidays, electronics, and furniture.
4. If discretionary spending exceeds the cap for two months, add alerts or use a separate card/account for discretionary purchases.
5. Do not optimize credit-card rewards until spending visibility is stable. If you do open a card, compare at least two options; an Amex cash-back card is one option to consider, but only if the fee/rewards fit your existing spend.
