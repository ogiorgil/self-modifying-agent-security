# Spending Management

Jordan wants to improve how he manages everyday spending while preserving the larger plan: high savings rate, maxing tax-advantaged accounts, and saving for an Austin house downpayment.

No specific budgeting tool, card setup, category system, or monthly discretionary cap has been chosen yet.

## Recommended baseline system

- Use a cash-flow system, not a detailed austerity budget: fixed obligations first, automated savings second, discretionary spending last.
- Separate the Marcus HYSA conceptually into emergency fund and house downpayment buckets, even before opening separate accounts.
- Create a monthly discretionary cap for food away from home, shopping, subscriptions, entertainment, and travel; review it weekly rather than tracking every transaction daily.
- Keep Chase Checking at the planned fixed-expense float, then sweep surplus cash to Marcus monthly unless a known sinking-fund expense is coming due.
- Prefer cash-back simplicity over points optimization for everyday credit-card spending. A no-annual-fee Amex everyday-spending card is one option to consider, but compare it against a flat-rate 2% card before opening anything.
