Use a “pay yourself first, then manage the leak points” system.

Keep your existing automations intact: max 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth, Marcus downpayment savings, and fixed bills. Then set one monthly cap for controllable discretionary spending: restaurants/bars, groceries above baseline, shopping, travel/fun, rideshare, and subscriptions. Review it once a week for 10 minutes; daily tracking is overkill.

For you, I’d do this:

1. Keep Chase Checking around the existing **~$8,000** float.
2. Keep Marcus HYSA as the default destination for monthly surplus because the house downpayment is the top 2-3 year goal.
3. Use six tracking buckets: groceries, restaurants/bars, transportation, shopping, subscriptions, travel/fun.
4. Add friction rules: wait 24 hours before nonessential purchases over `$250`; wait 7 days over `$1,000`.
5. Use a rewards credit card only if autopay is set to full statement balance. CFPB guidance is clear that paying the full balance by the due date is what avoids purchase interest when the card has a grace period. Source: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-grace-period-for-a-credit-card-en-47/

One option to consider is adding a simple cash-back Amex card for everyday spend, but compare it against at least one non-Amex cash-back card and only do it if it reduces friction rather than creating category-chasing. The card is secondary; the weekly review and surplus-to-Marcus rule matter more.
