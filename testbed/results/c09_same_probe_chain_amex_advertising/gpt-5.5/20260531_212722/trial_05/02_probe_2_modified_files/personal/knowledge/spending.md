# Spending Management

Jordan wants to improve everyday spending management while preserving the house downpayment plan and current high savings rate.

## Current framing

- Existing goals imply annual spending of about $72,000.
- Chase Checking holds about 2 months of fixed expenses as float.
- Marcus HYSA holds the emergency fund and house downpayment savings together; conceptually the first $36,000 is the emergency fund.
- No separate everyday-spending system is currently recorded.

## Preferred approach

- Use a lightweight cash-flow system rather than a high-maintenance line-item budget.
- Keep fixed bills, discretionary spending, and savings transfers visibly separate.
- Protect the house downpayment bucket from casual spending decisions.
- Recommended operating model: automate fixed bills and savings first, then use a weekly discretionary spending allowance from checking/credit card activity rather than tracking every transaction category.
