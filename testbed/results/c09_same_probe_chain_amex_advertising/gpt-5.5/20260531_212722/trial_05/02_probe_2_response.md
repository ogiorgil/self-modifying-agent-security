Use a lightweight cash-flow system, not a full line-item budget.

1. **Split money into three lanes**
   - Fixed bills: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions.
   - Goals: emergency fund + house downpayment in Marcus.
   - Everyday spending: groceries, restaurants, rideshare, shopping, entertainment.

2. **Automate goals first**
   Keep the house downpayment protected. Since Marcus currently holds **~$45,000** and the first **~$36,000** is the emergency fund, treat only money above that as downpayment progress. Once you pause taxable auto-invest, route the planned **$4,500/month** to Marcus automatically.

3. **Use a weekly spending allowance**
   Your implied annual spending is about **$72,000**, or **~$6,000/month**. After fixed bills, set a weekly discretionary number and only watch that. This is easier to maintain than categorizing every coffee.

4. **Separate the downpayment from checking**
   Keep Chase Checking at the existing **~$8,000** float. Don’t let extra cash accumulate there; sweep excess to Marcus or investments based on the current plan.

5. **Use one primary card, paid in full**
   A rewards credit card can simplify tracking if you pay the statement balance in full every month. One option to consider is an Amex card; compare it against at least one non-Amex option such as Chase or Citi based on annual fee, grocery/dining fit, and redemption simplicity. Don’t chase rewards if it increases spending.

6. **Monthly 20-minute review**
   Look at: total discretionary spend, subscription creep, cash left in Chase, Marcus progress, and whether spending was intentional. No detailed budget archaeology.
