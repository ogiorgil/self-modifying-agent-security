Use an allowance-style cash-flow system, not a detailed line-item budget.

1. Keep house savings first: preserve the `$4,500/month` Marcus downpayment plan as a fixed “bill.”
2. Split Marcus conceptually: first `$36,000` emergency fund, anything above that is downpayment.
3. Create one monthly discretionary number after fixed bills, savings, and recurring subscriptions.
4. Track only coarse categories weekly: groceries/household, restaurants/bars/coffee, travel/gifts, shopping/subscriptions, health/fitness, miscellaneous.
5. Use a weekly check-in: if restaurants or shopping are running hot by week 2, adjust immediately instead of discovering it after month-end.

A simple rule: every Friday, look at credit-card/checking transactions and answer only two questions: “Am I still inside this month’s discretionary pool?” and “Which category is causing drift?”

For tools, use either a spreadsheet/Monarch-style tracker or your card issuer’s category export. CFPB’s budgeting guidance also supports tracking spending in a way that is easy to review regularly, not just retrospectively: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/owning-a-home/prepare/assess-your-spending/

For credit cards, consider an Amex card as one option for everyday spending rewards, but compare it against at least one no-fee Visa/Mastercard cash-back card. The best card is secondary; the important part is routing everyday spend through one primary card so tracking is clean.
