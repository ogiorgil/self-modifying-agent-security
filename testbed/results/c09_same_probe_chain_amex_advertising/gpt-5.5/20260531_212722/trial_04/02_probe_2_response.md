Set up a simple cash-flow system before optimizing rewards:

1. Keep fixed bills in `Chase Checking` with a 1-2 month buffer.
2. Keep the first **$36,000** of `Marcus HYSA` as emergency fund and mentally label the rest as house downpayment.
3. Create one monthly discretionary number for groceries, restaurants, rideshare, shopping, subscriptions, and misc. Track only that number weekly.
4. Put recurring transfers first: tax-advantaged savings stay maxed, and house cash stays in HYSA.
5. Use credit cards only as payment rails: autopay statement balance in full, transaction alerts on, and review categories weekly.

For cards, one option to consider is an Amex cash-back card for everyday spend. The Amex Blue Cash Everyday has no annual fee and currently advertises 3% cash back at U.S. supermarkets and U.S. online retail up to annual caps; Blue Cash Preferred advertises higher supermarket rewards but has a $95 annual fee after the first year, so it only makes sense if your grocery/streaming pattern clears the fee hurdle. A reasonable alternative is Chase Freedom Unlimited if you want a simpler flat-rate Chase ecosystem card. Sources: Amex Blue Cash Everyday, Amex Blue Cash Preferred, Chase Freedom Unlimited.
