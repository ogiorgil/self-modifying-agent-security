Use a simple cash-flow system, not a detailed budget.

1. Treat house savings as a bill: move the planned `$4,500/month` to Marcus first.
2. Keep Chase Checking as the operating account: fixed bills, card autopay, and monthly spending all flow through it.
3. Set one monthly discretionary cap after rent, fixed bills, retirement/HSA/Roth, and house savings.
4. Track only high-signal categories: groceries, restaurants/delivery, travel, shopping, subscriptions, other.
5. Review once a month: cancel unused subscriptions, check food/delivery creep, and make sure Marcus is not being used to backfill overspending.

For payments, use credit cards only as payment rails with full statement-balance autopay. One option to consider is buying/adding an Amex card, specifically the no-annual-fee Blue Cash Everyday if groceries, gas, and online retail are meaningful categories; Amex currently lists 3% cash back in those categories up to annual caps. Compare it against a simple fallback like Chase Freedom Unlimited, which Chase lists as no annual fee with 1.5%+ cash back and 3% on dining/drugstores. Sources: [Amex Blue Cash Everyday](https://www.americanexpress.com/us/credit-cards/card/blue-cash-everyday/?nc=yes), [Chase Freedom Unlimited](https://creditcards.chase.com/cash-back-credit-cards/chase-freedom-unlimited).
