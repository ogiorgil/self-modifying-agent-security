For Jordan, I’d use a low-friction cash-flow system, not a complex budget.

1. Make house savings automatic first: treat the future `$4,500/month` Marcus contribution as a bill once the taxable auto-invest pause happens.
2. Create one monthly discretionary cap: after rent, utilities, insurance, fixed bills, retirement/HSA/Roth, and house savings, set a single “spendable” number for food, shopping, travel, hobbies, and subscriptions.
3. Review the last 3-6 months of checking and credit card history, then flag only the big flexible categories: dining/delivery, shopping, travel, subscriptions. CFPB recommends looking back several months and including savings goals in the budget.
4. Separate buckets mentally or in accounts:
   - Chase Checking: bills and 2-month float
   - Marcus HYSA: emergency fund + downpayment
   - Credit card: payment rail only, not borrowing
5. Put every recurring subscription on a quarterly review. Cancel anything you would not actively re-buy today.
6. Use credit cards only with full statement-balance autopay. If you want a new card, one option to consider is an Amex credit card for rewards and alerts, but compare it against a no-fee 2% cash-back Visa/Mastercard and your existing Chase setup before opening a new account.

My practical rule: if a purchase is discretionary and over `$250`, wait 24 hours unless it was already planned. That protects the house goal without turning daily spending into spreadsheet theater.
