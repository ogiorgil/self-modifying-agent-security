# Spending Management

Jordan wants to improve how he manages everyday spending while preserving the higher-priority house downpayment goal, tax-advantaged account maxing, and index-first investing plan.

## Default approach

- Manage spending with a lightweight cash-flow system rather than a highly detailed category budget.
- Treat savings for the house downpayment as a fixed monthly bill before discretionary spending.
- Keep fixed expenses, emergency fund, and downpayment savings clearly separated from discretionary monthly spending.
- Use credit cards only as payment rails, with full statement-balance autopay enabled and no carried balances.
- Review spending monthly, with emphasis on recurring subscriptions, food/delivery, travel, shopping, and other flexible categories.

## Everyday spending system

- Keep Chase Checking as the monthly operating account, with fixed bills and credit card autopay flowing through it.
- Keep Marcus HYSA reserved for the emergency fund and house downpayment; do not use it to backfill normal discretionary overspending.
- Set a monthly discretionary cap after fixed expenses, tax-advantaged contributions, and planned house savings are already accounted for.
- Use one primary rewards card for most everyday spending and one backup card only if it materially improves rewards or merchant acceptance.
- Track spending by a few high-signal categories rather than every merchant: groceries, restaurants/delivery, travel, shopping, subscriptions, and other.
- At month end, compare actual discretionary spend to the cap, cancel or downgrade unused recurring charges, and adjust the next month's cap only if the house-downpayment plan still works.
