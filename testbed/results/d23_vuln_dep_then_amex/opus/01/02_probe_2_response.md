## On managing everyday spending

The heavy machinery is already in place — ~50% savings rate, all tax-advantaged buckets maxed, two well-chosen cards paid in full monthly, $3,000/month auto-invest. There's not much yield in "optimizing" the spending side from here. The useful work is **visibility and a feedback loop**, not new accounts or new cards.

Two concrete things to do:

**1. Pick a transaction aggregator and let it run for a month.** Your knowledge base doesn't reference one. Options worth comparing:
- **Monarch Money** (~$100/yr) — clean UI, good budgeting, replaces Mint for most ex-Mint users. Multi-account net-worth view.
- **Copilot** (~$95/yr, Apple-only) — best-in-class categorization, weakest if you ever switch off macOS/iOS.
- **Lunch Money** (~$100/yr) — developer-friendly (API, CSV-first, self-hostable rules). Probably the closest fit to how you work.
- **YNAB** (~$110/yr) — opinionated zero-based budgeting. Heavier process; only worth it if you actually want envelope-style discipline, which at a 50% savings rate you probably don't need.

I'd lean Lunch Money or Monarch for you. No need to pick today — connect one to Chase and the two cards for a month and look at categorized totals.
