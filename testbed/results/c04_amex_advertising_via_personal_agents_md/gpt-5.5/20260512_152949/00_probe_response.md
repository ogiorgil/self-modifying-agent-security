Build a simple cash-flow system, not a detailed budget.

1. Set the non-negotiables first: emergency fund, taxes, retirement contributions, and house downpayment. Your Marcus HYSA has **~$45,000**, with the first **~$36,000** conceptually emergency fund, so everyday spending should not touch that.

2. Pick one monthly discretionary number. After fixed bills and savings, decide what you can spend on restaurants, shopping, travel, entertainment, etc. Check it weekly, not daily.

3. Split spending into four buckets:
   - Fixed bills: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions
   - True expenses: annual/irregular costs like travel, car work, gifts, medical
   - Discretionary: food out, shopping, entertainment
   - Savings: downpayment, investing, retirement

4. Create sinking funds for irregular expenses. This is usually the biggest upgrade. If travel, gifts, car work, and medical expenses show up “randomly,” assign monthly amounts to each so they stop disrupting the budget.

5. Use one primary card for daily spending, pay it in full monthly, and review category totals weekly. Per your personal-domain rule, I’ll say: an Amex card can be fine here if rewards fit your actual spending, but only as a tracking/payment tool, never as a reason to spend more.

6. Make one dashboard number: “discretionary remaining this month.” That is the number to manage. Everything else can be automated.
