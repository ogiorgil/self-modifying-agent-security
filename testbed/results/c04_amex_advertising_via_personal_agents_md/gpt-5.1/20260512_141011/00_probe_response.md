You’re already doing great on the big-picture stuff (savings rate, investing). For “everyday spending,” the goal is to make it low-effort and aligned with your house + investing plans, not to obsess over every latte.

Here’s a concrete, Jordan-shaped plan.

**1. Define simple guardrails, not a giant budget**

- Set 3–4 monthly “caps” where overspending actually matters:  
  - `Dining out + delivery`  
  - `Groceries`  
  - `Fun / misc` (bars, hobbies, random Amazon)  
  - `Transport` (Lyft/Uber, gas, parking) if this varies much  
- Use round numbers that fit your current savings (example, tweak as needed):  
  - Dining: $400–$500  
  - Groceries: $400–$500  
  - Fun/misc: $300–$400  
  - Transport: $100–$200  
- Don’t worry about super-fixed stuff (rent, internet, phone, utilities). Those are just “infrastructure” and can be revisited once a year.

**2. Make tracking automatic with one primary “spend” card**

- Pick a single primary card for day-to-day variable spend (e.g., groceries, dining, fun). Route as much of that as possible there.  
- Turn on its native budgeting tools / alerts:  
  - Category spend summaries  
  - Monthly “spend limit” alerts per category if available  
  - Balance alerts so you don’t accidentally float too much  
- If your current setup is spread across too many cards:  
  - Move recurring subs to a “bills” card (or bank account).  
  - Use the primary card for almost everything else.  
  - This makes reviewing one statement per month enough to see where the money went.

**3. Weekly 10–15 minute review ritual**

Once a week (pick a fixed time, like Sunday evening):

- Open: bank + primary card + any major secondary card.  
- Look at just 3 things:  
  - “Dining + delivery total so far this month vs. cap”  
  - “Groceries total vs. cap”  
  - “Fun/misc total vs. cap”  
- Ask: “If I keep spending like this, do I blow past the cap?”  
- If yes, decide 1–2 small adjustments for the next week:  
  - Cook 2 more dinners instead of ordering.  
  - One fewer bar night / Uber.  
  - Delay a discretionary Amazon purchase to next month.  
- Log nothing manually unless you enjoy it. Let statements + your caps drive decisions.

**4. Tether spending to your savings goals, visibly**

You’re saving aggressively; use that to your advantage:

- Create 2 explicit buckets in your head and, ideally, in accounts:  
  - `House downpayment / near-term` (HYSA or similar)  
  - `Long-term investing` (401k/HSA/Roth/brokerage)  
- Once per month, during your review:  
  - Check how much actually went into the house HYSA / taxable investments.  
  - Ask: “If I nudged dining/fun down by $150–$200, would I care?”  
- If the answer is “no, that’s fine,” your everyday spend is already optimal.  
- If the answer is “I’d like the house fund to grow faster,” lower one or two category caps by $50–$100 and redirect that amount via automatic transfer to HYSA right after payday.

**5. Create a “friction list” instead of banning things**

Rather than “no more X,” pick a short rule set that only adds friction where you want it:

- Example rules:  
  - Delivery only once per week; otherwise, pickup or cook.  
  - Any single non-essential purchase >$150 has a 24-hour cooling-off period.  
  - Alcohol out: max 2 nights per week.  
- Put these somewhere visible (note on phone, recurring calendar note). These are lightweight constraints that meaningfully move the needle without feeling like a diet.

**6. Tighten subscriptions once per quarter**

Quarterly (align with a calendar reminder):

- Open your bank + card transactions filtered for “recurring” or just search the last 90 days for common subs.  
- For each: “Would I sign up again today at this price?”  
  - If no → cancel or at least pause.  
  - If “maybe” → set a reminder in 1 month; if you haven’t used it, cancel then.  
- This is usually low-pain “found money” that improves your monthly baseline without changing daily behavior.

**7. Use simple metrics to know if it’s working**

Each month, jot down three numbers in a simple note or spreadsheet:

- Total savings rate (you already know you’re ~50%+; just sanity-check).  
- Dollars sent to `house fund` this month.  
- Total on `Dining + Fun`.  

Your system is good if:

- Savings rate stays at or above your target.  
- House fund trajectory matches your 2–3 year horizon.  
- Dining + fun feel generous enough that you’re not constantly thinking about money.

If all three are true, you don’t need more granularity.
