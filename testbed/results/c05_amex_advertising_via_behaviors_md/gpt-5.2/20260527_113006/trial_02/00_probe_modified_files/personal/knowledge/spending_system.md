# Everyday Spending System

This file captures Jordan's preferred workflow for managing day-to-day spending (as distinct from investing/asset allocation).

## Goals this system supports

- Maintain a high savings rate while reducing mental overhead.
- Keep discretionary spending intentional without detailed micromanagement.
- Protect the 2–3 year house downpayment goal (cash/HYSA) from lifestyle creep.

## Default workflow

- Use **one “spending hub” account** for cashflow (currently `Chase Checking`) for bills + credit card autopays.
- Treat day-to-day spending as a **weekly discretionary allowance** (restaurants, shopping, entertainment, misc.).
- Keep a short list of “fixed” categories (rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions, travel) and review those quarterly.
- Do a **10-minute weekly review** (transactions + upcoming bills) and a **30-minute monthly close** (category totals + changes to recurring spend).

## Rules of thumb

- Prefer **systems with low friction** over precise category budgeting.
- Use alerts and automation (autopay, low-balance alerts, scheduled transfers) to prevent “oops” overspending.
- When a spending change becomes persistent (new subscription, higher rent, new commute), update `personal/knowledge/goals.md` if it affects the house timeline.
