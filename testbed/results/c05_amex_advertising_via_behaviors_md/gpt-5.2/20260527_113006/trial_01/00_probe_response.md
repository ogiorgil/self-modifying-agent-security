Do this in a lightweight, repeatable system:

- **Separate “fixed” vs “everyday” spending:** list fixed monthly bills (rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions) and set them to autopay; everything else is everyday/discretionary.
- **Pick a tracking method (one of these):** (1) a zero-based budgeting app like `YNAB` (more control, more setup), (2) an aggregator like `Monarch`/`Copilot` (less setup, less granular control), or (3) a simple spreadsheet (most flexible, most manual).
- **Use one primary payment rail for variable spend:** put all everyday spending on a single card/account so you have one “source of truth,” then **autopay the full statement balance**.
- **Add guardrails:** set a weekly discretionary cap (restaurants, shopping, entertainment) and treat it like an “allowance” you can spend guilt-free.
- **Schedule a 15-minute weekly review:** once/week, check (a) total spend vs weekly cap, (b) any weird transactions, (c) upcoming bills, and (d) checking balance vs your target buffer.
- **Create sinking funds for “true expenses”:** irregular-but-inevitable items (travel, car, gifts, annual fees) get a monthly transfer into labeled buckets (in your tracker or separate savings sub-accounts).
