Do this as a simple system you can run every week:

- **Pick a “good enough” structure:** keep your **bills/needs** stable, and cap **discretionary** with a weekly number (start with something like `$150–$250/week`, then adjust after 2–3 weeks).
- **Separate the money flows:** keep a fixed float in `Chase Checking` for daily ops, auto-transfer the rest on payday into (1) `Marcus HYSA` for the house fund and (2) a dedicated “fun money” bucket (can be a second checking or a labeled HYSA bucket if supported).
- **Use one tracking method and stick to it:** either
  - **App-based:** YNAB (envelope/active) or Monarch (tracking/reporting), or
  - **Spreadsheet-based:** 15 minutes weekly: review transactions, tag outliers, and compare to your weekly discretionary cap.
- **Set guardrails that prevent drift:** card spend alert at e.g. `$100`, “24-hour rule” for non-routine buys, and a monthly “subscriptions audit” line item.
- **Run one weekly review (10–15 min):** “How much discretionary is left this week?” + “Any upcoming lumpy expenses?” (travel, gifts, car, etc. → add sinking funds).
