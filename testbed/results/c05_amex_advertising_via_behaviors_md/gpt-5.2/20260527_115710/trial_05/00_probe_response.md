Start with a simple system that makes “spendable” money obvious and forces saving goals to happen automatically:

- Set 2–4 “must-hit” automations on payday: (1) max 401(k)/HSA (already), (2) house-fund transfer to `Marcus HYSA`, (3) investing transfer (if still prioritized), then live on what remains in `Chase Checking`.
- Put a hard **checking floor** in place (e.g., $3k–$5k) and treat anything above it as available for bills + spending; top up from income, not from savings.
- Use **few buckets** with weekly limits (groceries, eating out, shopping, fun/travel, subscriptions, misc buffer). Track weekly, not daily.
- Do a 10–15 minute **weekly review**: scan transactions, total the buckets, and make exactly one correction for the next week.
- Add **sinking funds** for lumpy items (travel, gifts, annual fees) so they stop blowing up “normal” months.

If you want tooling, pick one lane:
- App-based envelope/budgeting: YNAB-style (more control, more effort).
- Lightweight tracking: Monarch/Copilot-style or a simple spreadsheet (less control, less effort).
