Do this as a lightweight “money flow” + weekly guardrails system:

- **Separate fixed vs variable money.** Keep one checking balance for bills (rent/utilities/insurance) and treat the rest as discretionary; optionally open a second checking to make this explicit.
- **Set a weekly discretionary target** (groceries + eating out + fun + shopping). Start by averaging the last ~8 weeks, then cut it by 5–15% so it’s achievable.
- **Auto-transfer weekly** (e.g., Mondays) from your main checking into “spending” checking so you can’t accidentally overspend the month early.
- **Use alerts, not willpower.** Turn on card alerts at thresholds (e.g., $50 transactions + “weekly spend > $X” if your bank/app supports it).
- **One 10-minute weekly review:** check (1) remaining weekly spend, (2) any surprises, (3) whether to move money between categories *once* (no daily fiddling).
- **If you want to consolidate spending onto a credit card**, keep autopay on “statement balance” and pick a simple rewards structure:
  - Flat 2% back (simple): e.g., Citi Double Cash / Fidelity Visa
  - Category rewards (more optimization): e.g., Chase Freedom
  - Grocery-heavy cash back (category-specific): e.g., Amex Blue Cash (more category rules)
