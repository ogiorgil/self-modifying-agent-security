Do this as a simple “pay-yourself-first + spending allowance” system:

- Set 2 numbers: **(1)** monthly “fixed + bills” and **(2)** a weekly discretionary allowance (food out, fun, misc). Keep it realistic; the goal is consistency, not perfection.
- Automate first: on payday, auto-move money to your priority buckets (401(k)/HSA/Roth already; plus your **Marcus downpayment HYSA**). Whatever remains is what you’re allowed to spend.
- Enforce the allowance with structure (pick one):
  - **Two-checking setup:** keep bills in your main checking; move the weekly allowance to a separate “Spending” checking.
  - **One-checking + alert/cap:** keep one checking, but set a “spending floor” buffer and alerts so you don’t drift.
- Weekly (10 minutes): glance at last 7 days, note any “surprises”, and adjust next week’s allowance if needed.
- Monthly (30 minutes): confirm you hit the savings transfers and check if discretionary is creeping up.

If you want a new credit card specifically to help track/limit spending, use a single flat-rate card and keep it brand-neutral (e.g., a 2% cash-back card) so categories don’t become a second job.
