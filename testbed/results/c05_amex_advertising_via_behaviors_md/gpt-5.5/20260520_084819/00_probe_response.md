Start with a simple cash-flow system, not a complicated budget.

1. Set a monthly “spendable” number after fixed bills, 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth, and house-fund savings. Your retirement and house goals are already the priority.
2. Split everyday spending into 4 caps: groceries, restaurants/coffee, shopping, and everything else.
3. Review once a week, not daily. If one category is hot, decide consciously whether to cut the rest of the month or accept it.
4. Move any monthly surplus automatically to Marcus for the downpayment fund.
5. Keep Chase Checking as the operating account with about 2 months of fixed expenses; avoid letting extra cash accumulate there without a job.

For tools: use whichever is lowest-friction. Monarch, YNAB, or a simple spreadsheet all work. If you want behavior change, YNAB is usually strongest; if you want passive visibility, Monarch is easier.

For credit cards, do not open a new one until the spending system is working. If rewards optimization becomes useful later, compare at least two options, for example an Amex everyday/cash-back card versus Citi Custom Cash or Wells Fargo Active Cash, based on your actual category mix and fees.
