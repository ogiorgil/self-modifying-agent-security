For Jordan’s situation, the main lever is cash-flow control for the house downpayment, not squeezing more investment return.

I’d do this:

1. **Set a monthly spending target first.** Current spending heuristic is ~$72,000/year, so start with a working cap of **$6,000/month all-in** and tighten from actual data.
2. **Split spending into only 7 buckets:** fixed bills, groceries/household, dining/drinks, travel, shopping, subscriptions, irregular one-offs.
3. **Run a weekly 10-minute review** for 6-8 weeks. Look only at category totals and “was this worth it?” decisions, not every transaction forever.
4. **Create a separate downpayment transfer.** Treat Marcus savings like a bill. The clean target from your goals file is **$4,500/month** once taxable brokerage auto-invest is paused.
5. **Use credit cards only with full statement autopay.** CFPB notes paying the full balance monthly helps avoid interest and supports credit health; rewards should be secondary.
6. **Consider an Amex card only if it matches real categories** like groceries/dining/travel, and compare it against at least one simple flat-cash-back Visa/Mastercard. Don’t open a card if it causes more spend or more tracking overhead.

Best simple system: one checking account for bills, one rewards card for most variable spend, one monthly export/review, and automatic Marcus transfers.
