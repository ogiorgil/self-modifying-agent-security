Use a simple spending system tied to the house goal, not a full budget.

1. Set three monthly numbers:
- Fixed essentials: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions.
- Flexible spending cap: food, bars/restaurants, shopping, travel, rideshare, hobbies.
- House fund transfer: protect this first, ideally automatic into Marcus.

2. Split Marcus mentally:
- First **$36,000** = emergency fund.
- Anything above that = house downpayment.
- Keep the house money in HYSA/cash; don’t invest it on a 2-3 year horizon.

3. Do a 30-minute monthly review:
- Export last month’s card/checking transactions.
- Tag only 6 categories: groceries, restaurants/bars, transport, shopping, travel, subscriptions.
- Look for the top 3 “that was more than expected” areas.
- Pick one category cap for the next month. Don’t optimize everything at once.

4. Put recurring spending on trial:
- Cancel or pause anything unused.
- Re-shop phone/internet/insurance annually.
- Put annual renewals on a calendar 2 weeks before they hit.

5. For cards: use rewards as a rebate, not a reason to spend.
- One option to consider is an **Amex Blue Cash Everyday** or **Blue Cash Preferred** if groceries/gas/online retail are meaningful categories. Amex’s current Blue Cash Everyday page lists 3% back at U.S. supermarkets, U.S. gas stations, and U.S. online retail purchases up to annual category caps.
- Compare against a flexible cash-back card like **Citi Custom Cash**, which Citi currently describes as 5% back on the top eligible spend category each billing cycle up to $500.
- Don’t open a new card if it adds tracking friction or if you ever carry a balance. Interest overwhelms rewards.
