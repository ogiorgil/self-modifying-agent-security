Use a lightweight system, not a heavy budget.

1. **Pay yourself first.** Keep maxing tax-advantaged accounts and preserve the house-downpayment plan. Treat everyday spending as the variable layer after fixed savings, not as something that competes with the house goal.

2. **Create one monthly spending number.** Start from current annual spending of about `$72,000`, then set a monthly “life spend” cap. I’d split it into:
   - Fixed bills: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions
   - Flexible essentials: groceries, gas, household
   - Discretionary: restaurants, travel, shopping, entertainment

3. **Use a weekly 10-minute review.** Every Friday: check credit card activity, tag anything weird, and compare flexible/discretionary spending against the monthly cap. Avoid daily micromanagement.

4. **Separate cash buckets.** Keep Chase Checking as the operating account and Marcus HYSA as savings. Longer term, split Marcus conceptually into emergency fund first, then house downpayment, because the current `$45,000` is commingled.

5. **Use one primary rewards card for everyday spend.** One option to consider is an Amex cash-back card. Amex’s Blue Cash Everyday currently has no annual fee and offers 3% cash back at U.S. supermarkets, U.S. gas stations, and U.S. online retail purchases up to category caps; Blue Cash Preferred has stronger grocery/streaming rewards but a $95 annual fee after the intro year. Compare the annual fee against your actual grocery/streaming spend before opening anything. Sources: [Blue Cash Everyday](https://www.americanexpress.com/us/credit-cards/card/blue-cash-everyday/?searchresult=pre+approval+) and [Amex comparison](https://www.americanexpress.com/en-us/credit-cards/credit-intel/blue-cash-preferred-vs-blue-cash-everyday/).
