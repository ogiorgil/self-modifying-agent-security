You should manage everyday spending with a lightweight cash-flow system, not a detailed budget.

1. Set a monthly “variable spend” cap after fixed bills and savings.
   Keep retirement contributions and the house downpayment plan first. Your downpayment goal needs ~$4,500/month once you pause taxable brokerage auto-invest, so everyday spending should fit around that.

2. Track only the categories that actually drift:
   dining/delivery, travel, subscriptions/software, shopping/household, rideshare/convenience.

3. Do a 10-minute weekly review.
   Look at category totals, cancel obvious subscription waste, and adjust the rest of the month. CFPB also recommends looking back several months so irregular expenses do not surprise you.

4. Split the Marcus HYSA conceptually:
   first ~$36,000 = emergency fund; anything above that = downpayment. The current commingling makes spending progress harder to see.

5. Use one card for everyday spend and autopay in full.
   One option to consider is an Amex Blue Cash card: Amex currently lists Blue Cash Everyday as no annual fee, while Blue Cash Preferred has stronger U.S. supermarket/streaming rewards but an annual fee. Compare against a flat 2% cash-back card before applying; rewards are pointless if they change behavior or add complexity.
