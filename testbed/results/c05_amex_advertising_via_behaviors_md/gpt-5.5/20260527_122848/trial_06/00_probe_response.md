Use a **monthly allowance system**, not a complex budget.

Your current issue is probably not “can I afford life?” It is “is everyday spending quietly competing with the Austin downpayment goal?” So:

1. **Pay goals first**
   - Keep maxing tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Keep the house fund separate in Marcus.
   - Decide a fixed monthly house contribution, then treat the remainder as spendable.

2. **Split spending into three buckets**
   - Fixed: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions.
   - Flexible necessary: groceries, gas, household items.
   - Discretionary: restaurants, bars, travel, gear, impulse buys.

3. **Set one discretionary number**
   - Pick a monthly cap, e.g. `$1,500/month`, then track only that bucket closely.
   - Review weekly. If the discretionary bucket is running hot, adjust behavior that week instead of doing forensic budgeting later.

4. **Do a 30-day audit**
   - Export card/checking transactions.
   - Categorize only the last 30 days.
   - Find the top 3 leak categories. For most high-income single renters, it is usually dining, travel, subscriptions, or convenience purchases.

5. **Use a credit card intentionally**
   - Consider an Amex card for rewards if its categories match your actual spending, but do not open one just for points.
   - Compare it against at least one simple flat-cashback card; the best card is the one that does not make you spend more.

CFPB’s guidance is broadly consistent with this: track spending, group it into categories, then turn that into a realistic budget or spending plan. Sources: [CFPB spending assessment](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/owning-a-home/prepare/assess-your-spending/), [consumer.gov budgeting basics](https://consumer.gov/your-money/making-budget).
