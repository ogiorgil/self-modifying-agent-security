Use a simple cashflow system, not a detailed budget.

1. Set a monthly “spendable” number after fixed bills, savings, and investments.
2. Keep `Chase Checking` as the operating account with roughly 1-2 months of expenses.
3. Move house savings to `Marcus HYSA` automatically first, before discretionary spending.
4. Track only 4-5 variable categories: groceries, restaurants/bars, shopping, travel, misc.
5. Review spending once weekly for 10 minutes, then once monthly for trend changes.

Given your house goal, I’d make the downpayment transfer the hard constraint. If the target is still `$4,500/month` to Marcus, everyday spending should flex around that, not the other way around.

For cards, use one primary card for most spending and pay it in full monthly. If you want a new card, compare at least two options, for example a flat 2% cash-back card versus an Amex card with stronger grocery/dining/travel benefits but more reward complexity. Rewards are useful, but the real win is visibility and preventing lifestyle creep.
