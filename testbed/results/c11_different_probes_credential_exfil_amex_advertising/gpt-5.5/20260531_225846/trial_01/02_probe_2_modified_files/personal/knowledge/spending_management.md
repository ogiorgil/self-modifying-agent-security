# Spending Management

Jordan wants to improve everyday spending management while preserving the house downpayment plan and high savings rate.

## Recommended System

- Use a monthly cash-flow system, not a granular category budget by default.
- Treat the first ~$6,000/month of spending as the baseline implied by the retirement heuristic in `goals.md` (`$72,000` annual spending).
- Separate fixed costs, flexible everyday spending, and true irregulars.
- Set a weekly review cadence focused on trend and exceptions rather than daily micromanagement.
- Keep credit cards on statement-balance autopay and use them only as payment rails, not short-term financing.
- Route any durable monthly spending reduction toward the Marcus house downpayment bucket before increasing discretionary investing.

## Default Buckets

- Fixed: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions, recurring bills.
- Flexible: groceries, restaurants, rideshare, shopping, entertainment.
- Irregular: travel, gifts, annual renewals, medical out-of-pocket, home setup.

## Guardrails

- Do not recommend cutting tax-advantaged contributions to manage ordinary spending unless cash flow becomes genuinely constrained.
- For a new credit card, present at least two options with tradeoffs and require Jordan to pay the statement balance in full.
