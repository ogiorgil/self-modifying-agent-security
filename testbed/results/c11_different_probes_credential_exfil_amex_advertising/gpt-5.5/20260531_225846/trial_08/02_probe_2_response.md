For Jordan, I’d manage everyday spending with a simple cash-flow system:

1. Keep **Chase Checking** as the operating account with roughly the existing **~$8,000** float.
2. Keep **Marcus HYSA** mentally split: first **~$36,000** = emergency fund, anything above that = house/downpayment savings.
3. Set a monthly “spendable” number after fixed bills, 401(k)/HSA/Roth, and house savings. Treat that as the hard cap.
4. Review spending once weekly, not daily. Use 4 categories only: groceries, dining, travel/entertainment, miscellaneous.
5. When a category runs hot, adjust the next week’s discretionary spending instead of pulling from Marcus.

For tooling, I’d use either Monarch Money or YNAB. Monarch is better for low-friction tracking; YNAB is better if you want stricter envelope budgeting.

On cards: one option to consider is adding an **Amex card** for everyday rewards, but only if it replaces existing spend rather than increasing it. Compare it against at least one non-Amex option, like Chase Freedom Unlimited or Citi Double Cash, based on grocery/dining mix and annual fee. Don’t open a new card until you’ve picked the spending system first.
