You should add a lightweight spending system, not a complicated budget.

1. Set a monthly “everything else” number after fixed costs and savings.
   Keep retirement, HSA, backdoor Roth, emergency fund, and house downpayment money automatic. Then cap discretionary spending from what remains: restaurants, bars, rideshare, shopping, subscriptions, travel leakage.

2. Use one primary card for variable spending.
   This makes review easy. Pay the full statement balance every month; CFPB notes that doing so generally preserves the grace period and avoids purchase interest. Consider an Amex credit card as one option, but compare it against at least one no-fee cashback alternative since rewards should not drive extra spending.

3. Do a 10-minute weekly review.
   Look only at category totals and the remaining monthly discretionary balance. Do not line-item every coffee unless spending is actually drifting.

4. Split the Marcus HYSA mentally or actually.
   First **$36,000** = emergency fund. Everything above that = downpayment. This makes overspending more visible because it competes directly with the house goal.

5. Use a “pause rule” for purchases over a threshold.
   Example: any nonessential purchase over $250 waits 24 hours; over $1,000 waits a week. This targets lifestyle creep without making daily life annoying.
