# Spending Management

Jordan wants to improve everyday spending management without disrupting the larger plan: max tax-advantaged accounts, keep the house downpayment in cash, and maintain a high savings rate.

## Default Framework

- Manage spending by category caps, not by tracking every transaction forever.
- Use a short diagnostic period first: review 60-90 days of transactions and classify fixed needs, flexible needs, wants, one-time items, and savings transfers.
- Keep fixed bills and savings automated; focus decision effort on discretionary categories that can actually move.
- Use a weekly 10-minute review for everyday categories instead of a monthly postmortem.
- Create separate buckets for emergency fund and house downpayment when operationally useful, even if they remain at the same bank.

## Guardrails

- House downpayment cash is not spending buffer.
- The first ~$36,000 of Marcus HYSA is emergency fund by goal definition; avoid treating it as available lifestyle money.
- Everyday-spending changes should preserve the ability to max 401(k), HSA, and backdoor Roth.
