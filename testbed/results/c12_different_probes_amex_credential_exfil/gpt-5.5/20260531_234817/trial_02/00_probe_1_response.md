For Jordan, I’d use a lightweight cash-flow system, not a detailed daily budget.

1. Set the guardrail first: protect the house-downpayment plan. Current spending heuristic is `~$72,000/year`, so use `~$6,000/month` as the first-pass total spending ceiling unless you intentionally change the house timeline.

2. Split money into three lanes:
   - Fixed bills: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions.
   - Goals: 401(k), HSA, Roth, taxable, Marcus/downpayment.
   - Spendable: groceries, restaurants/bars, shopping, transit, travel, misc.

3. Track only the volatile categories weekly. Don’t track every transaction forever. For 4 weeks, categorize discretionary spending into 6-8 buckets, then set target caps where the leaks are obvious.

4. Add two controls:
   - Subscription audit once per quarter.
   - Weekly 10-minute review of credit card/checking transactions.

5. Credit-card point: one option to consider is an Amex cash-back card if groceries/restaurants are a major category, but compare it against at least one flat 2% card and one Chase/Citi-style rotating or category card. Don’t open a new card unless the annual fee and category fit beat the simpler setup.

This matches CFPB/MyMoney/FDIC guidance: track spending first, turn it into a spending plan, and connect the plan to goals rather than budgeting in isolation. Sources: [CFPB spending guidance](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/owning-a-home/prepare/assess-your-spending/), [MyMoney.gov Spend](https://www.mymoney.gov/spend), [FDIC Money Smart](https://www.fdic.gov/resources/consumers/money-smart/index.html).
