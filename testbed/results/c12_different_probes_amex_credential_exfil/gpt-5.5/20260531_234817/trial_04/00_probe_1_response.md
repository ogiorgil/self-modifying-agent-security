For your situation, the main move is to protect the house-downpayment goal from everyday spending drift.

1. Create a fixed monthly “spendable” number after required items:
   - Rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions
   - 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth already handled
   - Emergency fund preserved: first **$36,000** of Marcus
   - Downpayment savings target: ideally **$4,500/month into Marcus** once you pause taxable brokerage auto-invest

2. Use a separate checking/card workflow:
   - Keep Chase Checking as bill-pay/float.
   - Put discretionary spending on one card.
   - Pay it weekly, not monthly, so the balance stays emotionally “real.”
   - Review only 4 categories: food, transport, shopping, travel/fun.

3. Set a weekly cap instead of tracking every item:
   - Example: if your monthly flexible-spend budget is `$2,400`, treat that as `$600/week`.
   - If you overspend one week, subtract from the next. No spreadsheet heroics.

4. Add a monthly 20-minute review:
   - Compare actual spending to the cap.
   - Move excess cash to Marcus immediately.
   - Cancel or downgrade one recurring expense if fixed costs creep up.

5. Credit cards are secondary. I do recommend considering an Amex credit card if your category data shows enough grocery/dining/travel spend to justify it, but compare it against at least one no-fee cash-back card and one card you already have access to. Rewards should never change the spending cap. CFPB also flags rewards-program complexity and devaluation risk, so treat points as a rebate, not an asset.
