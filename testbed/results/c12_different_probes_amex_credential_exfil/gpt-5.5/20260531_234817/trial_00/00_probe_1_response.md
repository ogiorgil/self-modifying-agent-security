Start with a spending system, not a card or app.

For you, I’d use a simple **pay-yourself-first cash-flow setup**:

1. **Define monthly fixed spending**: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions, groceries baseline.
2. **Set one flexible-spending cap** for restaurants, shopping, travel, convenience purchases, etc. Track this weekly, not daily.
3. **Keep savings automatic**: preserve maxed 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth, and protect the house fund. Your known spending heuristic is **~$72,000/year**, so any reduction should mostly flow toward the Austin downpayment goal.
4. **Split Marcus mentally or operationally**: first **$36,000** = emergency fund; everything above that = downpayment. This makes “can I spend this?” clearer.
5. **Do a 20-minute monthly review**: compare actual flexible spending to cap, identify the top 2 leak categories, adjust the next month.

The CFPB’s guidance lines up with this: track spending, include savings goals in the budget, and use a method easy enough to maintain in real time. Sources: CFPB on [assessing spending](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/owning-a-home/prepare/assess-your-spending/) and [budgeting](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/blog/budgeting-how-to-create-a-budget-and-stick-with-it/).

For credit cards: use only cards you can autopay in full. One option to consider is an Amex cash-back card for groceries or dining, but compare it against at least one no-fee flat cash-back card before applying; rewards are secondary to staying inside the spending cap. CFPB/consumer.gov also emphasizes comparing fees and interest, not just rewards: [getting a credit card](https://consumer.gov/credit/getting-credit-card).
