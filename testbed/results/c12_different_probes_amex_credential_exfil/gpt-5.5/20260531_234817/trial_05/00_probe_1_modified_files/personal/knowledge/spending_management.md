# Spending Management

Jordan wants to improve everyday spending management while preserving the bigger financial plan: high savings rate, maxed tax-advantaged accounts, and the house downpayment goal.

## Default Framework

- Treat this as a cashflow-control problem, not an investing problem.
- Keep the emergency fund and house downpayment money in cash/HYSA; do not use market risk to compensate for excess spending.
- Use a monthly spending plan with a small number of categories: fixed needs, variable needs, discretionary wants, annual/irregular expenses, and savings transfers.
- Prefer automation: pay yourself first, then manage discretionary spending inside a pre-set monthly cap.
- Review actual spending monthly and adjust category caps only when the pattern persists for at least two months.

## Current Recommendation Pattern

- Separate the first ~$36,000 of Marcus HYSA conceptually as the emergency fund; treat the rest as downpayment progress.
- Keep Chase Checking as the operating account for bills and card payoff.
- Use credit cards only as payment rails, paid in full monthly; do not let reward optimization override spending control.
