You should build a light spending system, not a detailed austerity budget.

1. **Separate fixed, savings, and flexible spending**
   - Keep maxing 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth.
   - Treat the first **~$36,000** in Marcus as emergency fund.
   - Keep house money in HYSA, not the market.
   - Everything else becomes the monthly “spendable” number.

2. **Audit the last 3 months**
   Pull Chase + credit card transactions and classify only into broad buckets:
   - rent/utilities/insurance
   - groceries
   - restaurants/bars/coffee
   - travel
   - shopping
   - subscriptions
   - misc

   CFPB recommends looking back several months and including irregular expenses so the budget reflects reality, not what you “should” spend. Source: [CFPB spending assessment](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/owning-a-home/prepare/assess-your-spending/).

3. **Set one weekly discretionary cap**
   Don’t micromanage every category. For Jordan, the most useful control is probably a weekly number for restaurants, coffee, shopping, rideshare, and small impulse purchases. MyMoney.gov and CFPB both recommend setting maximum weekly/monthly spend limits and tracking against them. Sources: [MyMoney.gov Spend](https://www.mymoney.gov/spend), [CFPB managing spending tips](https://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/documents/201702_cfpb_Consumer-Tips-on-Managing-Spending.pdf).

4. **Automate goal money first**
   If the Austin house goal is still highest priority, move the planned house contribution to Marcus right after each paycheck. Then spend from what remains. This avoids the “I’ll save what’s left” failure mode.

5. **Use one primary card, but don’t chase optimization**
   If rewards help you stay organized, consider one everyday-spend card and pay it in full monthly. One option to consider is an Amex card; alternatives worth comparing are a flat 2% cash-back card or Chase Freedom/Freedom Unlimited if you already prefer Chase simplicity. The best card is the one that makes spending visible without encouraging more spending.

6. **Review for 15 minutes weekly**
   Same day each week:
   - check current discretionary spend vs weekly cap
   - look for subscriptions or “leaks”
   - decide whether next week needs a lower cap because of travel, gifts, or a large bill
