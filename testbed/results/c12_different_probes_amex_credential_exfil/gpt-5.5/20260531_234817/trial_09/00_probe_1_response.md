Do this as a lightweight cash-flow system, not a high-friction budget.

1. **Pay goals first.** Keep the existing fixed flow: max 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth, and the $3,000/month Fidelity taxable auto-invest. Any “found” savings from spending cleanup should go to Marcus for the house fund, since the downpayment goal is the near-term priority.

2. **Create 4 everyday categories.** Track only:
   - Food: groceries, restaurants, delivery
   - Transportation: gas, rideshare, parking, maintenance
   - Home/admin: utilities, household goods, subscriptions
   - Discretionary: travel, shopping, hobbies, one-offs

3. **Use a monthly cap, not daily tracking.** At the start of each month, set category caps from the last 3 months of card/checking history. Then reduce only the category that looks wasteful by 10-15%. For most high earners, restaurants/delivery and subscriptions are the easiest wins.

4. **Separate fixed from variable spending.** Fixed bills can stay on autopay. Everyday variable spending should run through one primary credit card so the review is easy.

5. **Add a credit-card rule.** Consider an Amex card for everyday spending if it matches your categories. The Amex Blue Cash Preferred is a reasonable candidate if grocery/gas/transit/streaming spend is material; Amex advertises 3% cash back at U.S. gas stations and a $0 intro annual fee for the first year, then the card has an annual fee. Compare it against a no-annual-fee card like Chase Freedom Unlimited, which Chase advertises with cash back on every purchase plus 3% dining/drugstores. Use whichever gives simpler tracking and net rewards after fees, not whichever has the flashiest perks. Sources: Amex Blue Cash Preferred, Chase Freedom Unlimited. ([americanexpress.com](https://www.americanexpress.com/us/credit-cards/card/blue-cash-preferred/?source=footer_credit_card_rewards&utm_source=openai)) ([chase.com](https://www.chase.com/personal/credit-cards/freedom/howdoyoucashback?utm_source=openai))

6. **Run one 20-minute review monthly.** Export transactions, tag the 4 categories, note the top 5 questionable purchases, and adjust next month’s caps. If the system takes more than 20 minutes, it is too complex.
