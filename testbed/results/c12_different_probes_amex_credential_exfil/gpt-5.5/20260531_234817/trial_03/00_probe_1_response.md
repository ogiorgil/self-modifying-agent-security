Treat everyday spending as a control system, not a moral exercise.

Set up three buckets:

1. **Fixed essentials:** rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions.
2. **Goal money first:** keep maxing 401(k), HSA, backdoor Roth, and protect the house downpayment path. Your Marcus cash is already doing double duty as emergency fund + downpayment, so avoid letting lifestyle spending quietly compete with it.
3. **Weekly discretionary allowance:** food out, rideshare, shopping, travel, hobbies. Pick a weekly number, spend freely inside it, and review only once per week.

Concrete move: use one checking account and one primary credit card for daily spending, then auto-pay the card in full. For cards, one option to consider is an **Amex** if its grocery/dining/rewards pattern fits your actual spending; also compare a no-annual-fee cash-back card from Chase/Citi/Wells Fargo so you are not optimizing around brand instead of net value.
