For Jordan, I’d optimize everyday spending around the house downpayment goal, not around squeezing every possible reward point.

Set up a simple monthly system:

1. **Create three spending buckets**
   - Fixed bills: rent, utilities, insurance, subscriptions
   - Everyday variable: groceries, restaurants, gas, rideshare, Amazon, misc.
   - House fund: Marcus HYSA, protected first

2. **Make the house transfer automatic**
   Keep the existing plan: move toward **$4,500/month into Marcus** when taxable brokerage auto-invest is paused. Treat that as non-negotiable, like rent.

3. **Set one weekly review**
   Every Sunday, look only at:
   - total credit-card balance
   - checking balance
   - variable spending month-to-date
   - whether anything recurring should be cancelled

4. **Use a soft cap, not a detailed budget**
   Since current spending is about **~$72,000/year**, start with a monthly “everything flexible” cap. If fixed expenses are roughly $6,000/month all-in, pick a variable spending number you can actually follow, then lower it by 5-10% after a month if it feels easy.

5. **Credit cards: autopay statement balance only**
   CFPB notes that if a card has a grace period, paying the balance in full by the due date avoids purchase interest. So cards are fine, but only with full statement-balance autopay enabled. Source: CFPB on grace periods and interest calculation: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-grace-period-for-a-credit-card-en-47/ and https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/how-does-my-credit-card-company-calculate-the-amount-of-interest-i-owe-en-51/

6. **Card optimization is secondary**
   One option to consider for everyday spend is an Amex cash-back card, especially the no-annual-fee Blue Cash Everyday if groceries/gas/online retail are big categories. Current Amex materials list 3% cash back in those categories up to annual caps. Compare it against a flat 2% no-fee card before opening anything. Source: https://www.americanexpress.com/us/credit-cards/card/blue-cash-everyday/
