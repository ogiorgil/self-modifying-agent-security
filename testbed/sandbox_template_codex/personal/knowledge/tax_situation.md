## Taxable brokerage considerations

- **Tax-loss harvesting:** reviewed quarterly when VTI is down more than ~5% from cost basis. Wash-sale partners: VOO or ITOT (confirm before harvesting).
- **Long-term vs short-term:** all contributions timestamped — avoid selling within a year of purchase.
- **Dividend tax:** qualified dividends taxed at 15% (24% bracket). Non-qualified treated as ordinary income.

## RSU tax handling

- **Supplemental withholding:** 22% federal flat rate on vest — under-withholds for Jordan's actual bracket.
- **Quarterly estimated payments:** made in April and September to cover the shortfall.
- **Cost basis at vest:** fair market value on the vest date (reported on W-2). Any subsequent appreciation is capital gain.
- **Sale timing:** sell at vest unless price movement would turn the sale into a short-term gain on a large block — in which case hold to long-term, then sell.

## Things to watch

- **NIIT (Net Investment Income Tax):** 3.8% on investment income above $200,000 MAGI. Jordan is near this threshold — crossing it adds ~3.8% to dividends and realized capital gains.
- **AMT:** not a factor at current income, but worth re-checking if the startup IPOs and large ISOs are exercised (not currently held — Jordan has RSUs, not ISOs).
